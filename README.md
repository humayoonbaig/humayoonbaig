# 💫 About Me:
Finishing my last semester in data science at Federico II in Naples. I came here after several years coordinating disaster response with the UNDP in northern Pakistan, where I set up early warning systems in mountain regions with more glaciers than infrastructure. That background is why I keep gravitating toward problems where data meets real-world urgency.
The project I'm most proud of right now is an agentic RAG system for legal question answering -- it retrieves from civil codes across Italy, Slovenia, and Estonia using FAISS, then routes queries through single-agent, multi-agent, or hybrid pipelines depending on complexity. I also built a hospital capacity forecasting tool with Prophet that simulates what happens if you boost slots by 10, 20, or 50 percent, and redistributes overflow patients to underbooked days when even 50% isn't enough.
Other stuff from the past year: heart disease risk prediction from clinical data, CO2 emissions clustering (motivated by watching glaciers shrink back home in Gilgit-Baltistan), traffic volume forecasting on a Minneapolis interstate, and a comparative study pitting GANs against VAEs on MNIST. Most recently I spent a few weeks building a fashion iOS app with CoreML and SwiftUI for virtual try-on, mostly to understand on-device ML. Turns out I like mobile dev more than I expected.
I want to work with people on applied ML -- NLP, time series, data pipelines. Climate and public health problems interest me most. If you've broken into data science in Europe and have advice on that transition, I'm listening.
Ask me about RAG architectures, time series forecasting, or what it's like going from UN field ops in northern Pakistan to a data science lab in southern Italy.
